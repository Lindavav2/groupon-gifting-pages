Over 20,000 Adventure Deals Now Available as Gifts — And Germany Is Back After Three Years

For most of Groupon's history, the majority of third-party activity and experience deals — Viator tours, outdoor adventures, attraction tickets — could not be sent as gifts. A buyer who wanted to give a skydiving experience or a city walking tour had no way to do it through Groupon. That changed this week. Following a series of discussions with the Viator partnership team, Groupon confirmed that these experiences can be gifted without any issues at check-in. The result: over 20,000 adventure and outdoor activity deals are now enabled for gifting for the first time.

In the same week, Germany was cleared for re-enablement. Germany had been excluded from this category since 2023 — a legacy restriction that legal reviewed and confirmed is no longer needed. Engineering has the green light to enable German deals, with no operational changes required for merchants. Germany's top supply gap — thermal baths — is the single highest-opportunity category in any international market at an estimated $25,000 in additional annual margin at conservative improvement scenarios.

The main challenge ahead: buyers who select "send as a gift" on these experience deals are completing their purchase only about half as often as on regular Groupon deals. Understanding and improving that checkout experience is the next priority for the team.

NEWS ROOM

2,582 Deals Have a Fine Print Problem — 368 Could Be Opened for Gifts Right Now

A full scan of the live deal catalog turned up two issues. First: 368 deals that are currently blocked from gifting contain language in their terms suggesting the voucher can be used by anyone — these could be enabled immediately. Second: 2,214 deals that ARE set up for gifting contain "non-transferable" language in their fine print, meaning a merchant could technically use it to refuse a gift voucher. The next step is agreeing across Sales, Legal, and the catalog team on who corrects these and how — starting with the highest-revenue deals first.

Source: Asana · Gifting supply gaps · GI Log March 23, 2026

$1.1M Opportunity Mapped Across 479 Category/Market Gaps — Scoring System Next

The gifting opportunity analysis is complete: 479 high-priority combinations of deal category and market where gift sales fall significantly below what the same category achieves elsewhere. At a conservative 15% improvement, the incremental annual margin is $1.1M. The next step — currently being designed — combines this opportunity map with a deal quality scoring system to produce a ranked action list showing which specific deals to fix first and in what order. Once ready, the output goes to Sales and Supply for prioritization discussions.

Source: Asana · Gifting supply gaps — $1.1M M1+VFM · GI Logs March 24–27, 2026

EU Gift Card Distribution: One Contract Clause Away From Launch

Groupon is close to launching an entirely new revenue stream — selling Groupon-issued gift cards through EU retail and B2B networks, beginning with a pilot at iKiosk terminals in Germany and then scaling to over 130 European retail and corporate channels. The commercial agreement is at its final stage: one clause covering how financial losses are handled in a fraud scenario remains open. The Germany pilot has four deals selected and is ready to start the moment the contract is signed. The financial target at medium scale is over €8.4M in annual sales and €1.9M in margin.
