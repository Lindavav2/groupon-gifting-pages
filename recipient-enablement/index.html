Buy as Gift is a different product, different behavior, different methods.

2. Buy as Gift vs Send as Gift — two different behaviors
Source: Keboola deal_level_gift_metrics ✅
🛍 Buy as Gift — 66.6% US · 70%+ EU
→
Buyer downloads or prints the voucher themselves — gives it physically, or goes to the experience together with the recipient
→
No digital recipient flow — Groupon doesn't know who the recipient is
→
Naturally dominant in experience categories (spa, activities, restaurants) — the "going together" scenario
→
This is not a failure. It's a different use case. Recipient tracking makes no sense here.
US: 1,553,636 units · EU: 69–72% of volume in FR/PL/DE/UK
📧 Send as Gift — 33.4% US · ~30% EU
✓
Buyer enters recipient's email/phone → Groupon sends a digital notification
✓
Recipient "claims" = adds voucher to Groupon account → tracking is possible
✓
CRM interventions, push notifications, expiry reminders — all relevant only here
✓
Of the 779K sent: 46.5% claimed → 29% then make a purchase (Dennis data ⚠️)
US: 779,453 units = actual recipient enablement pool
💡 Why EU appears to have a lower "claim rate" even with the same website/app EU has 70%+ Buy as Gift (vs US 67%) — 3–5pp more volume goes outside the digital recipient flow. When measuring claim rate on total gift units (including Buy as Gift), EU will inevitably come out lower. Category mix is practically identical (95%+ L1-Local in both). This is not a product failure — it's a behavior difference. Before looking for causes in UI/UX, verify whether EU recipients who received a digital Send as Gift have a similar claim rate to US.

5. Weak spots — where we're provably losing value
🔴 Gift Card — broken funnel worth ~$1.2M NOB Gift Card vouchers: 4.4% claim rate (vs average 46.5%). Known bug: uuid = nil on iOS/Android breaks the voucher without crediting Groupon Bucks. Redemption rate 16.3% for those who get past the bug. Source: Dennis Oct data ⚠️. This is a bug, not a product issue. Engineering ticket needed. Dennis as owner.
🔴 Post-send recipient care doesn't exist After a digital gift is sent (Send as Gift): the buyer gets no "Delivered / Claimed / Redeemed" status. The recipient gets no reminder if they don't claim. No follow-up after redemption. All post-send value is untapped — across 779K US Send as Gift units per year.
🟡 Cinema anomaly — 48% claim, 10% redemption Recipients claim the voucher but never show up to the cinema. Value loss somewhere between claim and redemption — could be expiry, forgetting, or poor communication about validity. Needs investigation.
🟡 December = biggest month, worst claim efficiency Blended claim rate drops to 41% in December (Dennis ⚠️) — likely because the Buy as Gift share grows (physical gifts under the tree). This is structural, not a product problem. Opportunity: expiry reminders triggered in December would capture January/February as the "claim window."
